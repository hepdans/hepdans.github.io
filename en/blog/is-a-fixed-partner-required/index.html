Is a fixed partner required?

You do not need a fixed partner for social Latin dances, namely Salsa, Bachata and Cha cha cha. As it can be understood from the name, they are “social” dances. It means that you do not need to prepare a choreography to dance, all you need is to improvise. The male partner will combine his dance figures with Salsa patterns and leads his partner.

If you have a fixed partner, your spouse for instance, should not hinder you from learning to dance. On the very contrary, if you always have someone to practice with you, that is great news! But you are aiming to improve your dancing skills, the more people you dance with, the better you will get. Different partners can help you improve in different ways and they may also inspire you.

Should you come to the class with a partner?

You can always come with a partner. But, it is not required. Since we switch partners during classes, you do not need to have a fixed partner. Having a fixed partner will give you the opportunity to practice more with that person. However, it might result in developing fixed dancing habits. If you are careful about this, dancing with a fixed partner can be to your advantage. These tips are for those who would like to actively dance at dance night events and other social events. Have fun dancing!
