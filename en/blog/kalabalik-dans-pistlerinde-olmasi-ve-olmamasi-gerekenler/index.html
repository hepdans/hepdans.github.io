DOs and DON’Ts of Crowded Dance Floors

DOs: For LA(Los Angeles) and NY(New York) style Salsa, it is Cross Body Lead style; namely you dance on a single line. If you stick to this line and consider the structure of the dance venue, the risk of hitting one another decreases.

DON’Ts: Do not stay on the cross body lead line and shift the combinations to different directions. Here, of course, men have a lot of responsibility, and they have to lead to stay on the line. Do not start dancing on a perpendicular line to the couple next to you without aligning yourself accordingly.

DOs: Small steps! We all know that big steps mess up and scatter our dance. Small steps will help us occupy less space on the dance floor and will decrease the risk of hitting others.

DON’Ts: Big steps. Dancing with big steps on a crowded dance floor will, of course, result in you hitting others.

DOs: This is especially for the ladies: Not using too many hand and arm movements. Keeping styling in moderation with adding a little spice to your moves such as body isolations that you can do in a tight space.

DON’Ts: Making big arm movements without caring about your surroundings.

DOs: For gentlemen: If you are in a tight space lead your partner with moves and turns as simple as possible. Trying to make advanced turns without hitting anyone while there are people around is too much for a social dance that you do just for the fun of it.

DON’Ts: Performing difficult turns without even letting your partner realize the direction of the move while not taking into account the tight space factor.

Long story short: Dance with your partner and your surroundings in mind, not solely your own pleasure. Always remember that everyone dances to have fun.

Additionally: As you can see, the most important factor here is the male partner’s lead. What you need to learn by heart is the techniques for leading and following.
